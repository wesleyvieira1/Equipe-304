Understand how the oceans influence combating climate change

Climate can be defined as the state of balance in a system of exchanges of thermodynamic properties between different compartments. The ocean plays a climate regulation role thanks to its heat capacity. It releases heat more slowly and helps balance the temperature differences between regions.

Rising temperatures cause ocean acidification, reducing their ability to absorb and retain carbon. As a result, marine life is negatively impacted and harmed by human actions, such as emitting tons of carbon dioxide (CO2) daily.

An example of this acidification occurred in American Samoa between December 2014 and February 2015, as shown in the image available on The Ocean Agency's website.
