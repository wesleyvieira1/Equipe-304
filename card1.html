What is the origin of oxygen?

One of the main functionalities of algae for society's life is its ability to convert carbon dioxide, much of it produced by humans themselves,
into oxygen, which is a vital gas for the population. This process will be described below, step by step, along with an image.

  1. Algae contain photosynthetic pigments, such as chlorophyll, which are located in cells. These pigments capture sunlight.
  2. Algae absorb carbon dioxide from the environment, which is one of the main reactants in photosynthesis.
  3. Using the energy of sunlight captured by chlorophyll, algae undergo a series of complex chemical reactions
    that take place in organelles called chloroplasts. These reactions convert carbon dioxide and water into glucose (sugar) and oxygen.
  4. The oxygen produced during photosynthesis is released into the environment as a byproduct. This oxygen is essential for the
    respiration of many organisms on Earth, including humans
